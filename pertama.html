Observation Of Can Waste

Waste is the remainder of a production process, or it can also be the remnants of human activities that are no longer valuable and useless. Various types of waste exist; one of them is solid waste. Examples of solid waste include used paper, food wrappers, cardboard boxes, used cans, used bottles, and many more. I am here observing used biscuit cans, the shape of which will be recreated.

Used biscuit cans are utilized as a storage rack for chargers and cable rolls. It can also be used as a place to store other items. The materials used to make this storage rack are very easy to find. The main ingredients are round and short biscuit cans, for example, a Monde Biscuit can. The other materials are scrap fabric (anything), PVC pipes cut approximately 2 meters (divided into 8 pieces). It also requires connecting pieces for the PVC pipes (can use rope and 4 pieces of cardboard that are rolled up), and a rope which is a round shape. The tools needed are a glue gun, scissors, marker, and pliers.

The steps to make a storage rack that is not too high and easy to practice. We need to layer the inside of the can with used fabric, and each can is given a round cardboard piece on the bottom of the can. If the inside of the can is already neat, then the outside of the can is covered with rope. The part of the can that is layered with rope will look attractive and neat.

Then, the ends of each PVC pipe are given connecting pieces and re-connected (with the remaining pieces of PVC pipe). The PVC pipe pieces that are already connected are then fitted to the cans, both the parts of the can that are still whole and the parts that are still scraps. After the PVC pipes and connecting pieces are placed, they are layered with rope, used fabric, and the biscuit cans... and the storage rack is finished.
