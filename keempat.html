Observation Of Can Waste


Used cans are leftover containers from drinks or food that are commonly found in the environment. This type of waste is classified as inorganic waste because it is made of metals such as aluminum, which are difficult to decompose in the soil. Used cans are usually cylindrical in shape with a hard surface.

Many people still throw used cans carelessly, which causes the environment to become dirty. If left unchecked, used cans can pose dangers such as injuring people or becoming a breeding place for mosquitoes, and they can damage the ecosystem, potentially causing organisms to become extinct.

However, used cans can be reused into useful items, such as plant pots, pencil holders, or other handicrafts that may have selling value. Waste that was originally thrown away and difficult to decompose can be transformed into something valuable.

Therefore, the public needs to be more concerned about managing used can waste. By recycling, we can keep the environment clean while reducing pollution. Let’s make use of recyclable waste and throw used cans in the proper waste bins so they do not pollute the environment.
